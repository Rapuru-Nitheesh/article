Kerala:The God's Own Country

- by Nitheesh

Culture and Tradition

Kerala is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, which includes classical art forms, festivals, and cuisine. Dance forms like "Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam" are iconic, blending storytelling with intricate gestures and vibrant costumes. Festivals such as "Onam, Vishu, and Thrissur Pooram "are celebrated with grandeur, reflecting the unity and vibrancy of its people. The cuisine is equally diverse, with dishes like appam, fish curry, and payasam showcasing the use of coconut, spices, and fresh local ingredients.

Climate

Kerala, located in the southern part of India, is renowned for its tropical monsoon climate, which is heavily influenced by its proximity to the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats. The state experiences warm and humid weather throughout the year, with lush greenery and vibrant ecosystems thriving under its consistent climate.The year in Kerala is marked by four distinct seasons. Summers, from March to May, are relatively hot and humid, with temperatures ranging between 28°C and 38°C. This season also witnesses pre-monsoon showers, locally known as "Mango showers" which provide some relief from the heat.Kerala receives heavy and sustained rainfall during this period, turning the state into a verdant paradise and revitalizing its rivers and backwaters.

Tourism

One of the highlights of Kerala’s tourism is its stunning natural beauty. The backwaters, particularly in "Alleppey" (Alappuzha) and "Kumarakom", offer a unique experience with houseboat cruises through tranquil lagoons and canals surrounded by swaying palms. The state’s hill stations, such as "Munnar, Wayanad, and Thekkady", are adorned with misty mountains, tea plantations, and cascading waterfalls. For beach lovers, Kerala boasts pristine stretches of sand like Kovalam, Varkala, and Marari, each offering a mix of relaxation and adventure. Wildlife enthusiasts can explore sanctuaries like "Periyar and Wayanad", which are home to exotic flora and fauna.Kerala’s unique blend of natural splendor, cultural richness, and wellness opportunities make it a must-visit destination.

Kerala's Cuisine

Kerala’s cuisine is a rich and flavorful tapestry, reflecting the state’s tropical climate, cultural diversity, and abundant natural resources. The dishes are characterized by the liberal use of coconut in various forms—grated, as milk, or oil—along with aromatic spices like black pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and turmeric, which give the food its distinctive flavors. Rice is the staple, forming the base for meals and appearing in dishes like "appam, puttu, and idiyappam", often paired with spicy curries or coconut-based stews. Seafood plays a prominent role, with fresh fish, prawns, crab, and mussels forming the heart of many dishes, such as the iconic "karimeen pollichathu" (pearl spot fish grilled in banana leaves), "chemmeen curry" (spicy prawn curry), and fish moilee (mild fish curry in coconut milk).
